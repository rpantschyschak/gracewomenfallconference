A Seminar on Racial Reconciliation

In the Old Testament, righteousness refers to something fixed and the way it should be … a norm, a standard, by which other things can be measured. In this context it means conforming to how God says it should be. Justice in many ways puts the standard of righteousness in practice by making things right, fixing a problem, intervening where things have gone wrong, confronting and correcting those who are in the wrong and delivering those suffering the injustice.

Connect – Learn – Grow is a phrased approach to full reconciliation to Christ. To connect, learn, and grow, opens us up to taking first steps to address personal prejudices, biases, and racism in the church and the nation through biblical teaching, education, and application. As professed believers and followers of Jesus Christ, it is our duty to do take a stand for justice, for all.

Understanding How to Apply "Our Bodies Are a Temple" to Our Daily Lives

1 Corinthians 6:19-20 states that our bodies are a temple. We are made in the image of God and in this seminar, we will walk you through the spiritual, mental and physical aspects of health. We will also dispel some of the common misconceptions about how to take care of yourself so that you can live your life filled with His peace and inner strength.

Encouragement for Survivors and Friends in the #metoo and #churchtoo Movement

What does it mean to be made “In His Image”? The statistics tell us that one woman is sexually assaulted every 45 seconds, that 1 in 3 women have been victim to sexual, dating, and/or domestic violence. So how can we encourage each other to find hope in God’s truth for us? As survivors, we struggle with overcoming the burden of that identity label. As a church, we struggle with providing the needed support and friendship for victims. In this seminar we will learn about how God’s truth and scripture is the core foundation for us re-discovering our identity in Him, and how to respond when someone shares their story with you.

A Foundational Bible Seminar

Our knowledge, understanding, and application of Scripture is a necessary and crucial part of our Christian walks. However, the Bible can seem overwhelming and confusing, and we can get caught in the trap of misinterpretation and misapplication. In this session, you'll discover what it really means to be made in the image of God and learn how to approach Scripture in light of the truth that we are His image bearers.

Seminar Leader | Cait Brown
Cait Brown

I grew up between Philadelphia and Washington, D.C. and graduated from the University of Maryland where I studied in Biology, Anthropology, and French. I’m in a relationship and am a digital marketing manager for a technology company in Northern Virginia.

For the first 25 years of my life, I identified as an atheist and would often use my educational background in science to (harshly) support my views and criticize Christians and their beliefs. In 2018, after many years of struggling with mental illness, I came to a much healthier place, but still experienced this unrelenting feeling that my life was incomplete and empty, and something huge was missing; I just couldn't figure out what it was. "Out of the blue," I decided to attend a church to see if I could find any hope or answers. The first time I went to Grace, I sat in the very last seat of the very last row so that I could exit quickly because I just knew I would hate the service. Instead, I cried through that entire service as it perfectly spoke to a personal struggle I was in the midst of tackling. I have returned every week since (and now sit in the front row!). On October 28, 2018, three months after coming to Grace, by the grace of God I accepted Christ as my Savior after the Lord opened my eyes to His truth and gave me a new heart.

My passion has become seeking and knowing God through the study of His Word, and at the end of last year I felt called to create a community for the purpose of seeing women come to know and love the Lord and His Son Jesus Christ. That community developed through Instagram, at @groundedingrace_, where I share Biblical encouragement, short devotionals, advice for reading the Word, and answer questions from people in all stages of their faith walk.
